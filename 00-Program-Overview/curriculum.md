# GRC Mentorship Programme — Full Curriculum Overview

## Programme Philosophy

This programme is built on one uncompromising principle: **you learn GRC by doing GRC**.

Every week you produce professional deliverables — risk registers, gap assessments, audit reports, board presentations, policies, vendor risk questionnaires — exactly as a GRC Analyst or Consultant would in a real organisation. By the end of 24 weeks, you have a portfolio of 15 completed, professional-grade projects that prove your competence to any employer or client.

The world is full of people who can talk about ISO 27001. Hiring managers want people who can produce an ISMS Scope Statement, a 93-control gap assessment, and a board-ready implementation roadmap on their first week in the role. This programme makes you that person.

The second principle is **career-first design**. Every topic is taught through the lens of "how does this skill help you get hired and perform excellently in your first GRC role?" Framework knowledge, regulatory understanding, technical skills, and professional communication — all developed in service of a single outcome: a job-ready GRC professional.

## Mentorship Sessions

Each week includes 2 structured mentorship sessions:

| Session | Format | Duration | Focus |
|---------|--------|----------|-------|
| Session A | Live call or recording | 90 min | Topic teaching, concept explanation, live exercise |
| Session B | Live review call | 60 min | Assignment feedback, quality review, interview practice |

**Mentor responsibilities:**
- Provide written feedback on every assignment (within 3 business days)
- Share real-world examples and case studies from professional experience
- Conduct mock interviews (Weeks 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24)
- Review GitHub portfolio quality and professional presentation
- Provide certification study guidance

## Phase-by-Phase Breakdown

### Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1–4)

**Learning philosophy:** Build the conceptual bedrock. Before you can conduct a gap assessment or audit, you must understand what GRC is, why risk management matters, how governance structures work, and how compliance obligations flow. These 4 weeks give you the vocabulary, frameworks awareness, and initial portfolio pieces that underpin everything that follows.

**Key deliverables:**
- GRC Landscape Analysis Report (Week 1)
- Enterprise Risk Register with heatmap (Week 2)
- Security Governance Framework and RACI (Week 3)
- PCI-DSS and GDPR Gap Assessment (Week 4)

**Mock interview:** End of Week 4 — tested on GRC fundamentals, framework awareness, and risk management basics.

---

### Phase 2: Frameworks in Depth (Weeks 5–10)

**Learning philosophy:** Go deep on each major framework individually. ISO 27001, NIST CSF, SOC 2, GDPR, policy development, and vendor risk are the core technical disciplines of GRC. This phase makes you genuinely expert in each, not just aware of them.

**Key deliverables:**
- ISO 27001 SoA and Gap Assessment (Week 5)
- NIST CSF 2.0 Maturity Assessment with radar chart (Week 6)
- 10-Policy Security Policy Library (Week 7)
- Vendor Risk Assessment Questionnaires ×3 (Week 8)
- SOC 2 Readiness Assessment (Week 9)
- GDPR DPIA and RoPA (Week 10)

**Mock interview:** End of Week 8 — tested on ISO 27001, GDPR, and vendor risk knowledge.

---

### Phase 3: Audit and Control Testing (Weeks 11–16)

**Learning philosophy:** Shift from designing controls to testing them. This phase covers the assurance dimension of GRC — internal audit, control testing, BIA, incident response, and metrics. These are the skills that make GRC professionals valuable beyond documentation — they can independently verify that security programmes actually work.

**Key deliverables:**
- Internal Audit Programme and Access Control Audit Report (Week 11)
- Control Testing Workbook with Evidence Inventory (Week 12)
- Business Impact Analysis for 8 processes (Week 13)
- Incident Response Plan and 3 Playbooks (Week 14)
- Security Metrics Dashboard and Board Briefing (Week 15)
- CIS Controls v8 Assessment with Cross-Framework Mapping (Week 16)

**Mock interview:** End of Week 12 — tested on internal audit methodology, control testing, and ISO 19011.

---

### Phase 4: Advanced GRC (Weeks 17–20)

**Learning philosophy:** The skills of a senior GRC professional. Quantitative risk using FAIR, enterprise-scale risk programme design, multi-framework GRC programme management, cloud and AI governance, and career preparation. These weeks prepare you for the intermediate to senior GRC roles you will grow into.

**Key deliverables:**
- FAIR Quantitative Risk Analysis for 3 risks (Week 17)
- Unified Compliance Matrix and GRC Programme Charter (Week 18)
- CIS AWS Benchmark Assessment and AI Governance Policy (Week 19)
- Professional GRC Resume, LinkedIn Profile, and Mock Interview Pack (Week 20)

**Mock interview:** End of Week 20 — full 45-minute mock interview covering all topics + portfolio walkthrough.

---

### Phase 5: Capstone — NovaTech Corp (Weeks 21–24)

**Learning philosophy:** Synthesise everything into one complete, enterprise-grade GRC programme. The NovaTech capstone is the centrepiece of your portfolio — the deliverable that demonstrates you can build and present a real enterprise GRC programme from the ground up. This is the project that opens the door to your first GRC role.

**Client:** NovaTech Corp — 2,000-person UK fintech, £180M ARR, 5 million payment customers, FCA-regulated, PCI-DSS Level 2, GDPR obligations. No ISO 27001 or SOC 2. Three FCA findings. Series C investors requiring ISO 27001 before Series D.

**Key deliverables (4 weeks):**
- GRC Strategy, Governance Framework, Operating Model, Roadmap (Week 21)
- 30-Risk Register with FAIR Analysis + 15-Policy Library (Week 22)
- Unified Compliance Matrix + ISO 27001 Gap Assessment + Audit Programme + 5 VRAQs (Week 23)
- Security Metrics Dashboard + Board Presentation (20 slides) + GRC Maturity Assessment (Week 24)

**Final assessment:** 30-minute board presentation + 15-minute Q&A with mentor panel acting as NovaTech board.

## The GitHub Portfolio Standard

Your GitHub portfolio is your most powerful career asset. Recruiters and hiring managers reviewing your application will visit your GitHub before calling you for interview. It must look genuinely professional.

### Every Repository Must Have:

- ✅ Professional README.md: scenario description, your role, frameworks applied, deliverables, skills demonstrated
- ✅ All deliverables in correct folder structure — no empty folders
- ✅ No placeholder text: "Lorem ipsum", "[Your Name]", "TBD" — anywhere in any document
- ✅ Professional document formatting: version headers, document IDs, classification labels, review dates
- ✅ Framework references clearly cited in all documents (e.g., ISO 27001 A.5.18, GDPR Art. 33)
- ✅ Consistent file naming: Company-Deliverable-v1.0.md

### Portfolio Quality Checklist (Week 24 Final Review):

- [ ] 15+ projects with complete deliverables across all phases
- [ ] NovaTech Capstone is the flagship — most complete and most polished
- [ ] All projects have professional README.md explaining scenario and skills demonstrated
- [ ] GitHub profile has professional bio mentioning GRC specialisation

- [ ] 6 best repositories pinned

## GRC Career Landscape (2025)

### Demand and Growth

The UK cybersecurity skills shortage exceeds 50,000 unfilled roles. GRC represents approximately 25–30% of all cybersecurity job postings — making it one of the largest and most accessible entry points. Drivers of demand: increasing regulation (GDPR enforcement, FCA operational resilience, NIS2, DORA), growth in ISO 27001 and SOC 2 as enterprise requirements, and rising cost of cyber incidents.

### What Employers Are Looking For (Top 10 Skills, 2025)

Based on analysis of 500+ UK GRC job postings:

1. ISO 27001 — knowledge and implementation experience
2. NIST CSF — assessment and roadmap development
3. GDPR / UK GDPR — compliance programme management
4. Risk assessment — qualitative and quantitative methods
5. Security policy development — writing audit-ready policies
6. Internal audit — ISO 19011 methodology
7. Vendor / third-party risk management
8. SOC 2 — readiness and evidence management
9. Executive / board communication — translating risk into business language
10. GRC tooling — Vanta, ServiceNow GRC, RSA Archer, or equivalent

## Certification Pathways (Enhanced)

### Tier 1 — No Experience Required

| Cert | Body | Exam Cost | Study Time | Priority |
|------|------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) | CompTIA | ~£300 | 60–90 hrs | HIGH — pursue immediately |
| Cyber Essentials | NCSC | ~£300 | 15–20 hrs | Medium — UK-specific value |
| ISACA Cybersecurity Fundamentals | ISACA | Free | 10 hrs | Easy — free signal of ISACA engagement |

### Tier 2 — 6–18 Months Experience

| Cert | Body | Exam Cost | Study Time | Priority |
|------|------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| ISO 27001 Foundation | PECB / BSI | ~£400–600 | 30–40 hrs | HIGH — strong market signal |
| CIPP/E | IAPP | ~£600 | 60–80 hrs | HIGH if GDPR/privacy focus |
| ISO 27001 Lead Implementer | PECB | ~£1,200 | 80–100 hrs | HIGH — major salary boost |
| ISO 27001 Lead Auditor | PECB | ~£1,200 | 80–100 hrs | High — pairs well with Lead Implementer |

### Tier 3 — 3+ Years Experience

| Cert | Body | Experience Required | Exam Cost | Priority |
|------|------|--------------------|-----------| ---------|
| CRISC | ISACA | 3 years | ~£550 | High — risk management gold standard |
| CISA | ISACA | 5 years | ~£550 | High — IS audit gold standard |
| CGEIT | ISACA | 5 years | ~£550 | Medium — IT governance focus |

### Recommended Sequence for Programme Graduates

1. **During programme:** Study CompTIA Security+ (target: pass within 3 months of programme end)
2. **Months 1–6 in first role:** ISO 27001 Foundation (employer may fund)
3. **Months 6–18 in role:** ISO 27001 Lead Implementer or CIPP/E based on role focus
4. **Years 2–3:** CRISC (requires 3 years experience)
5. **Years 4–5:** CISA (requires 5 years experience)
