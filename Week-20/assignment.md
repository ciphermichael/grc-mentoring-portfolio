## Scenario Brief — Read This Carefully

You have completed 19 weeks of the GRC Mentorship Programme and built 14 portfolio projects. You have demonstrated competence across ISO 27001, NIST CSF 2.0, SOC 2, GDPR, CIS Controls, FAIR risk quantification, internal audit, vendor risk management, business impact analysis, incident response, cloud GRC, and AI governance. You are now ready to enter the job market as a junior-to-mid GRC Analyst.

This week's assignment is different — instead of client work, you are preparing yourself for the job market. The deliverables are your professional career assets: a polished resume, an optimised LinkedIn profile, a publication-ready GitHub portfolio, a rehearsed interview preparation pack, and a structured 12-month career development plan.

**Target roles you are applying for:**
- GRC Analyst (Entry–Junior)
- Information Security Analyst (with GRC focus)
- Compliance Analyst
- Risk Analyst (Cybersecurity)
- Junior GRC Consultant

**Important:** All deliverables this week are about YOU, not a fictional client. Write your actual resume, your actual LinkedIn profile content, and your actual career goals. This is the output of 20 weeks of work — make it excellent.

### Deliverable 4: Mock Interview Preparation Pack

**What It Is:** A structured preparation document for your next GRC interview.

**Section 1: Your "Tell Me About Yourself" Script (2 minutes)**
Write a script you can actually deliver. Structure:
- Who you are professionally
- What you've done (portfolio highlight — 2 sentences)
- Why GRC (genuine motivation)
- What you're looking for

Practice it until you can deliver it naturally in under 2 minutes.

**Section 2: STAR Answer Bank (10 Answers)**
Write full STAR-format answers for these 10 questions:
1. "Tell me about a time you identified a significant risk."
2. "Tell me about a time you had to explain a technical topic to a non-technical person."
3. "Tell me about a project where you had to manage multiple deadlines."
4. "Tell me about a time you made a mistake and how you handled it."
5. "Describe a project you're most proud of in your GRC portfolio."
6. "Tell me about a time you had to research something unfamiliar and deliver expertise quickly."
7. "Describe how you ensure quality in your GRC deliverables."
8. "Tell me about a time you disagreed with an approach — how did you handle it?"
9. "Give an example of a complex analysis you have produced."
10. "Describe a situation where you had to prioritise multiple competing demands."

Each STAR answer should be 150–200 words.

**Section 3: Technical Q&A Preparation**
Write concise, confident answers (3–5 sentences each) for 10 technical questions:
1. Walk me through ISO 27001:2022 structure
2. What changed in ISO 27001:2022 vs 2013?
3. What are the 6 NIST CSF 2.0 functions?
4. What is a Statement of Applicability?
5. What is GDPR Article 33 and what is the deadline?
6. What are the 4 risk treatment options?
7. What is the difference between SOC 2 Type I and Type II?
8. What is the Three Lines of Defense model?
9. Walk me through how you conduct a risk assessment.
10. What is the FAIR model?

**Section 4: Questions to Ask the Interviewer (10 Questions)**
Prepare 10 intelligent questions that demonstrate GRC knowledge and genuine interest:
Examples:
- "Which compliance frameworks is the team currently managing?"
- "Is there a dedicated GRC platform in use, or is compliance tracked manually?"
- "What does the onboarding process look like for a new GRC Analyst?"
- "What are the biggest GRC challenges the organisation is facing in the next 12 months?"
