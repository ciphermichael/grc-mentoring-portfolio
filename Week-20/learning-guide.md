GRC roles represent 25–30% of all cybersecurity job postings. Drivers: increasing regulation (GDPR enforcement, DORA, NIS2), growth in ISO 27001 and SOC 2 certifications as enterprise requirements, and the increasing cost of cyber incidents.

## Section 2: How to Build a Job-Ready GRC Profile

### The GRC Resume (2 Pages Maximum)

**What hiring managers look for (in order of importance):**
1. Relevant certifications (CompTIA Security+, ISO 27001 Lead Implementer, CISA)
2. Framework knowledge (ISO 27001, NIST CSF, SOC 2, GDPR — must be in the resume)
3. Specific GRC deliverables produced (risk registers, gap assessments, policies)
4. Industry experience (financial services, healthcare, and technology pay premium)
5. GRC tools (Vanta, ServiceNow GRC, RSA Archer, MS Purview)
6. Degree (relevant but secondary to certifications and portfolio)

**Resume structure:**
```
[NAME] — GRC Analyst | Cybersecurity Governance, Risk & Compliance
[LinkedIn] | [GitHub] | [Email] | [Location]

PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY (3 sentences)
"GRC professional with foundational expertise in ISO 27001:2022, NIST CSF 2.0, and GDPR compliance. 
Demonstrated ability to conduct risk assessments, build security policy libraries, and perform 
internal audits through a comprehensive 6-month structured GRC programme. Pursuing CISA certification."

CORE SKILLS
ISO 27001 | NIST CSF 2.0 | SOC 2 | GDPR | Risk Assessment | Internal Audit
Security Policy Development | Vendor Risk Management | Executive Reporting | GitHub

PORTFOLIO PROJECTS (most important section for career changers)
[Project 1]: ISO 27001 Gap Assessment — Conducted a 93-control gap assessment for a fictional SaaS company
  → Produced SoA, maturity scoring, and 12-month implementation roadmap
[Project 2]: Enterprise Risk Register — Built 25+ risk register using FAIR quantitative methodology
  ...

CERTIFICATIONS
CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) — [Year] / Pursuing
ISO 27001 Foundation — [Year] if completed

EDUCATION
[Degree, Institution, Year]
```

**ATS optimisation:** Include exact keywords from job descriptions: "ISO 27001", "NIST CSF", "GDPR", "risk register", "gap assessment", "compliance analyst", "GRC analyst", "information security".

### LinkedIn Profile Optimisation

**Headline** (most important line): Don't use job title. Use value proposition:
"GRC Analyst | ISO 27001 | NIST CSF | GDPR Compliance | Risk Assessment | Building cybersecurity portfolio"

**About section**: 3–4 paragraphs. Tell your story: why GRC, what you've built, what you can do, what you're looking for.

**Featured section**: Pin your GitHub portfolio link prominently.

**Skills section**: Add all 50 skills — they're searchable. Priority: ISO 27001, NIST CSF, GDPR, Risk Management, Information Security, Compliance, Internal Audit, Vendor Risk Management, Security Policy, SOC 2.

**Activity**: Post weekly about GRC topics — sharing insights from your learning, commenting on industry news, publishing portfolio project summaries. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards consistent activity.

**Connections**: Connect with GRC professionals, CISOs, compliance managers, and recruiters. Engage genuinely with their content.

### GitHub Portfolio Polish

Your GitHub portfolio is your most powerful career asset. Key quality criteria:

- **Repository README**: Each project README must tell a complete story: scenario, your role, frameworks used, deliverables produced, skills demonstrated.
- **Pinned repositories**: Pin your 6 best projects on your profile.
- **Commit history**: Active commit history signals active learning. Commit regularly.
- **Profile README**: Create a profile README (special repository named after your username) with your GRC specialisation summary, skills, certifications, and contact links.
- **File organisation**: Clean folder structure, professional file naming (no "final_v2_FINAL.xlsx").
- **No placeholder text**: Every document must be complete. Assessors reject portfolios with "Lorem ipsum" or "[Your name]" placeholders.

## Section 3: Interview Preparation

### Types of GRC Interviews

**Competency-Based Interviews**: "Tell me about a time you..."
- Designed to assess past behaviour as a predictor of future performance
- Use STAR format: Situation → Task → Action → Result

**Technical Knowledge Interviews**: "Walk me through ISO 27001..."
- Direct knowledge testing of frameworks, processes, terminology
- Reference your portfolio work — "When I conducted the ISO 27001 gap assessment for Xown Solutions, I..."

**Case Study Interviews**: "Here is a scenario — what would you do?"
- Tests analytical thinking and practical application
- Structure your answer: Understand the context → Identify the key issues → Apply the framework → Recommend and justify

**Portfolio Review Interviews**: "Walk me through your GitHub portfolio"
- Most powerful interview opportunity for career changers
- Prepare a 10-minute portfolio walkthrough in advance

### The Portfolio Walkthrough Script (10 Minutes)

Open with context (30 seconds): "My portfolio demonstrates 6 months of structured GRC learning through real-world scenarios. I completed 15 projects covering the full GRC lifecycle — risk assessment, policy development, internal audit, compliance mapping, and executive reporting."

Project 1 (2 minutes): Your strongest project. Walk through the scenario, what you produced, and what you learned.

Highlight breadth (1 minute): "Across the 15 projects I worked with ISO 27001, NIST CSF 2.0, SOC 2, GDPR, PCI-DSS, CIS Controls, and the FAIR quantitative risk model."

Capstone highlight (2 minutes): The NovaTech Corp capstone — "This was the flagship project simulating a complete enterprise GRC programme. I built a governance framework, risk register, policy library, compliance mapping, audit programme, vendor assessments, and board presentation."

Invite questions (30 seconds): "I'd be happy to walk through any specific project in more detail. Which framework or area would be most relevant to your team?"

## Section 4: Certification Pathways

### The Optimal Certification Sequence

**Phase 1 — No Experience Required (Start Now):**
| Cert | Body | Cost (approx.) | Study Time | Value |
|------|------|----------------|------------|-------|
| CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) | CompTIA | £300 | 60–90 hours | Entry-level signal; many job postings require |
| Cyber Essentials | NCSC | £300 | 20 hours | UK-specific; demonstrates baseline |
| CISM Foundation (ISACA) | ISACA | Free | 5 hours | Free; shows ISACA engagement |

**Phase 2 — 6–18 Months Experience:**
| Cert | Body | Cost (approx.) | Study Time | Value |
|------|------|----------------|------------|-------|
| ISO 27001 Foundation | PECB/BSI | £400–£600 | 30–40 hours | Strong signal for ISO 27001 roles |
| CIPP/E | IAPP | £600 | 60–80 hours | Essential for GDPR/privacy roles |
| ISO 27001 Lead Implementer | PECB | £1,200 | 80–100 hours | Premium; major salary boost |

**Phase 3 — 2–3 Years Experience:**
| Cert | Body | Cost (approx.) | Study Time | Value |
|------|------|----------------|------------|-------|
| CISA | ISACA | £550 | 150–200 hours | Requires 5yr exp; gold standard for audit |
| CRISC | ISACA | £550 | 120–150 hours | Requires 3yr exp; risk management gold standard |
| ISO 27001 Lead Auditor | PECB | £1,200 | 80–100 hours | Pairs with Lead Implementer for consulting |

**Recommended path for programme graduates:**
1. CompTIA Security+ — before job search (no experience required; 60 hours study)
2. ISO 27001 Foundation — in first 6 months of role
3. CIPP/E — if GDPR is primary focus
4. ISO 27001 Lead Implementer — once in role with experience
5. CRISC or CISA — at 3–5 years experience

## Section 5: Common Mistakes Beginners Make

1. **Applying before the resume is optimised.** Submitting a resume that doesn't contain the right keywords (ISO 27001, NIST CSF, GDPR) means the ATS rejects you before a human sees it.

2. **Not tailoring applications.** Every application should include a tailored cover letter referencing the specific role requirements. Generic applications have <5% response rate.

3. **Not preparing STAR format answers.** Competency interviews require structured answers. Without preparation, candidates ramble. Practice 10 STAR answers for common GRC interview questions.

4. **Hiding the portfolio.** Many candidates mention their portfolio but don't link to it prominently. The GitHub link should be in the resume header and the LinkedIn featured section.

5. **Undervaluing the portfolio.** Candidates who can't explain what they built, why, and what they learned make poor impressions. Know your portfolio deeply.

6. **Applying only to perfect-match roles.** Entry-level GRC roles rarely require everything. Apply if you meet 60–70% of requirements. The portfolio compensates for gaps in formal experience.

7. **Neglecting LinkedIn.** A significant proportion of GRC jobs are filled through LinkedIn recruiter messages. An optimised profile with regular activity gets inbound recruiter contact.

## Section 7: Instructor Mentorship Guidance

### Session A Focus (90 min) — Mock Interview

**Format:** 45-minute mock interview with debrief.

**Interview questions to ask:**
1. "Tell me about yourself" — test the 3-minute professional introduction
2. "Walk me through your GitHub portfolio" — 10-minute portfolio walkthrough
3. "What is ISO 27001 and when would you recommend it?" — technical knowledge
4. "Tell me about a time you identified a significant risk" — STAR format
5. "How would you explain GDPR to a non-technical CEO?" — communication skill
6. "Where do you see yourself in 3 years?" — career planning

**Debrief:** Structured feedback on: content knowledge, communication clarity, STAR structure, portfolio presentation, confidence.

### Session B Focus (60 min) — Portfolio Review

- Review resume with mentor: ATS optimisation check; formatting; portfolio prominence
- Check LinkedIn: Headline, About section, Featured section, Skills
- Review GitHub: README quality, pinned repos, profile README, commit history
- Build the 12-month career development plan together

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Candidate cannot explain any project in detail beyond what's in the README
- STAR answers lack specific results ("things improved" without quantification)
- Resume has ISO 27001 listed as a skill but candidate cannot explain the clause structure
- Portfolio has placeholder text in any document

## Section 8: Interview Preparation — The 50 Most Common GRC Questions

### Governance and Strategy
1. What does GRC stand for? What does each component mean?
2. Walk me through how you would build a security governance structure from scratch.
3. What is the Three Lines of Defense model?
4. What should a CISO include in a board presentation?
5. What is the difference between risk appetite and risk tolerance?

### Risk Management
6. How do you conduct a risk assessment?
7. What is the FAIR model?
8. What is inherent vs residual risk?
9. What are the four risk treatment options?
10. How do you quantify cyber risk in financial terms?

### ISO 27001
11. Walk me through ISO 27001:2022 structure.
12. What changed in ISO 27001:2022 vs 2013?
13. What is a Statement of Applicability?
14. What is the difference between Stage 1 and Stage 2 audits?
15. Name 10 Annex A controls and explain what they require.

### NIST CSF
16. Walk me through the 6 NIST CSF 2.0 functions.
17. What was new in NIST CSF 2.0 vs 1.1?
18. What is a CSF Profile?
19. What are the 4 implementation tiers?
20. How does NIST CSF map to ISO 27001?

### GDPR
21. What are the 7 GDPR data protection principles?
22. What are the 6 lawful bases for processing?
23. What is the 72-hour GDPR breach notification deadline?
24. What is a DPIA and when is it required?
25. What is a Record of Processing Activities?

### Compliance
26. What is PCI-DSS and who must comply?
27. What is SOC 2 and what are the 5 Trust Services Criteria?
28. What is the difference between SOC 2 Type I and Type II?
29. What are CIS Controls?
30. How do you manage compliance across multiple frameworks?

### Audit and Controls
31. Walk me through the internal audit lifecycle.
32. What is the difference between design effectiveness and operating effectiveness?
33. What are the 4 types of control testing evidence?
34. What is a control exception vs a material weakness?
35. What is ISO 19011?

### Vendor Risk
36. How do you tier vendors by risk?
37. What is GDPR Article 28?
38. What is a Vendor Risk Assessment Questionnaire?
39. What is a supply chain attack?
40. How do you conduct ongoing vendor monitoring?

### Operational
41. What is a Business Impact Analysis?
42. What is RTO vs RPO?
43. Walk me through incident response lifecycle.
44. What is GDPR Article 33?
45. What is a post-incident review?

### Career
46. Why do you want to work in GRC?
47. How do you stay current with regulatory changes?
48. What GRC tools have you used?
49. Tell me about your portfolio.
50. Where do you want to be in your GRC career in 5 years?
